## ABSTRACT
**Artificial Intelligence (AI)** is transforming healthcare by improving disease detection. In dermatology, AI analyzes skin images to identify cancer, enabling faster diagnoses. However, many AI systems perform less accurately on darker skin because they are trained primarily on lighter-skinned images. This creates an ethical challenge: while AI can save lives through early detection, it may also perpetuate health inequities.

This case study examines racial bias in AI skin-cancer diagnostic tools, focusing on ethical dilemmas between innovation and fairness. It explores how unbalanced training data can harm patients with darker skin, identifies affected stakeholders, and discusses solutions such as diverse datasets, fairness testing, and regulatory oversight.
