## Patients and Healthcare Users
AI can help doctors detect skin cancer earlier and more accurately, but it may work less well for darker skin tones. Learn how AI might be used in your care, what questions to ask about your diagnosis, and how your data is used. Understanding these issues helps you make informed decisions and advocate for fair treatment.

## Medical Professionals and Hospitals
AI can improve workflow, diagnostic accuracy, and patient care—but biased AI can also introduce errors, reduce trust, and increase workload. This section covers best practices for ethical AI use, integrating tools safely, and maintaining patient trust while using AI support.

## AI Developers and Policymakers
Developers and regulators play a key role in ensuring AI is fair, accurate, and accountable. Learn why diverse datasets, transparency, bias testing, and collaboration with clinicians are essential to prevent harm and promote equitable healthcare outcomes.
